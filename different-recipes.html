Recipe 1: Jollof Rice

Nigerian Style

The science of Jollof rice

Jollof rice is a West African dish comprising of rice, tomoatoes, and a stock base. It is consumed alll across Africa as a celebration food. Weddings, birthdays, and celebrations of birth all across Africa all feature a plate of jollof. Paired with a hearty chicken leg and a side of salad, this meal has amazed and tantalized tounges for centuries. A simple dish, jollof is easy to make (and even easier to mess up). Components of Jollof rice

Jollof rice has 3 basic ingredients:

        1. Tomatoes
        2. Rice
        3. Stock

In this recipe, you will learn how to make these three component of jollof

1. Tomatoes

The first step to making jollof rice is to cook down some Tomatoes. These don't necessarily have to be tomatoes, Vivian herself recomends a blend of tomoatoes and tatashe - a less sweet variant of bell pepper. First, cook down a small onion and some garlic until its translucent and fragrant. You'll know its ready when it starts smelling delicious! Afterwards, the tomatoe blend can be addded. The main point of the base, however, is to cook it down. The tomatoe mix shou ld be thoroughly dry and removed of all water. Any water in the base makes the rice sour and tasti ng of raw tomatoe.

Another important part is properly seasoning the base. Curry, thyme, buillion cubes, rosemary. Anthing in the cupboards will do. If the base has no salt, it'll be hard to introduce it once the rice has started. The base must be super seasoned.Meaning, it should taste too salty or sweet. Later, you will have to add water to it so it should be salty enough that diluting it will still give the rice flavour.

2. Rice

After the tomatoes have been cooked down and water has been added, you must add rice to your diluted base. You can use any basic rice you find in store, Vivian uses long-grain rice from superstore but any other can work in a pinch. The rice should be washed. The amount of water added into the mixture should be about 2 or 3 cups, depending on how much rice addded. Once the rice is added, you can add more water to make sure the water covers the rice with about half-a-finger of clearance on top.

It should be cooked for 20 minutes on medium-high heat until the water in near done. When the water is almost finished, it should be switched to medium-low heat until the water evaporates or the rice is soft. A little salt or pepper can be added now, to taste.

3. the Stock

This is the place with the most diveristy. Stock can be store-bought vegetarian or anything in the fridge. On one notable occasion, vivian took a dollop of stew she'd already made to make a stock for the ric e. Traditionally its beef, or chicken. Whenever you make meat or boil chicken save the stock in your fridge for later. Inthis recipe, Vivian used pork stock from her thanksgiving dinner. In the absence of stock, crushed up buillion cubes mixed with water works just as well. Gravy packets will also work. All the stock does is add another layer of flavouring onto the food and makes it taste that much nicer.

Jollof Rice Recipe:

Ingredients you'll need

  1. 1 Cup of tomatoe paste or blend
  2. A small onion, diced
  3. 2-3 cloves of garlic, crushed
  4. Salt and other seasoning, 3 tsps each
  5. 2 Cups of Cooking stock (not neccesary)
  6. 1 Cup of long-grain rice; washed
  7. 1 Cup of Water

Cooking instructions

Cook onions and garlic down in medium-low heat for 5-10 minutes or until mixture is fragrant and translucent.

Add tomato mixture. Stir slowly and minimally, only enough for the tomatoes to not burn. The goal is to cook the tomatoes down, so stirring continuosly will add to the cooking time. While tomatoes are cooking, add salt, pepper and other spices.

Season heavily, and cook tomoatoes for 10-15 minutes on medium-high heat. Tomatoe mix should be salty and savoury by the end

Add water, and stock if available until mixture is watery and diluted.Re-season to taste.

Add washed rice and stir mixture. Water should cover the rice with a few inches of space extra.

Leave pan covered for 10-15 minutes on medium-high heat until water is almost evaporated. Then, turn heat to medium low and constantly check water level and softness of rice until rice is of desired softness.
